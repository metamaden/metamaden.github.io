My education includes graduate-level courses in bioinformatics, biostatistics, and epidemiology. I received BA Biology from Reed College (2011) where my [thesis](https://www.reed.edu/biology/professors/srenn/pages/research/2011_students/sean/SM_thesis.html) quantified genetic diversity in cichlids (advisor Prof. Suzy Renn). In 2015, I began studying epigenetics in the context of gastroesophageal cancer risk as a Data Analyst assistant with [Grady Lab](https://research.fhcrc.org/grady/en.html) in the Clinical Research Division at Fred Hutch. I enrolled as a Ph.D. student at OHSU in Summer quarter 2018.

I possess over 5 years of full-time experience applying programming and computation for research. My first ever Bioconductor package, [`recountmethylation`](https://www.bioconductor.org/packages/devel/bioc/html/recountmethylation.html), a resource for accessing and analyzing cross-study compilations of DNA methylation array studies, is now available. I frequently program in the R, Python, and bash programming languages.

High-impact reproducible research is among Sean's key professional interests. He excels at cross-disciplinary collaborations, and enjoys presenting his research to diverse audiences. Whether you have an interesting computational problem or just want to discuss pursuing a career in bioinformatics, don't hesitate to reach out!
