About me

I grew up in and around the Pacific Northwest. I am a fun-loving vegetarian who is happily married to my life companion, Lindsay. We live near Portland, OR, (United States) with our cat Suki. When I'm not immersed in data science or programming projects, I love to read, cook, walk, hike, run, game, listen to music, and watch shows.

Professional experience

Data Analyst | Bioinformatics Researcher | Transcriptomics | Epigenomics | Informatics Analysis

I obtained my Ph.D. Computational Biology/Biomedical Engineering in 2022. I am a senior-level analyst in bioinformatics with expertise in transcriptomics, epigenomics, and informatics who is passionate about open-access development that combines scientific rigor with innovative solutions.

Highlights

Transcriptomics Expertise: I have deep understanding of transcriptomics that allows me to unravel complex gene expression patterns and identify critical regulatory elements.

Epigenomics Insights: My work in epigenomics sheds light on how modifications to DNA impact gene function, providing valuable insights for personalized medicine and disease research.

Informatics Analysis: I excel in analyzing large-scale biological datasets, extracting meaningful information, and translating it into actionable knowledge.

Open-Source Contributions: My commitment to the scientific community is evident through my contributions to the Bioconductor repository. I developed three open-source packages: lute, cellScaleFactors, and recountmethylation.

Software Development: My skills extend well beyond analysis. I designed interactive dashboards, streamlined workflows, robust pipelines, user-friendly applets, and responsive websites.

Data Science: I possess 8 years of experience applying a complement of analysis, statistics, reporting, and programming skills to carry out data science for research.
