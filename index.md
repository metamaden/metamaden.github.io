Sean is a bioinformatician, computationalist, and [3rd year Ph.D. candidate](https://www.ohsu.edu/people/sean-maden), Computational Biology, in the Biomedical Engineering Department at Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU). His advisor is Prof. Abhi Nellore, and his research affiliations include [PDXgx](https://github.com/pdxgx) (Thompson and Nellore lab groups) and the [recount](https://jhubiostatistics.shinyapps.io/recount/) team at Johns Hopkins (includes the Leek, Langmead, and Hansen lab groups).

Overlapping bioinformatics, biostatistics, and computation, Sean's research focuses on disease pathology, epigenetics, and biological aging. He has contributed integrative analyses of large genomics datasets in numerous publications, and he possesses expertise in whole-methylome analyses using DNA methylation arrays.

Sean's college education includes courses in liberal arts and evolutionary biology at Reed College, bioinformatics and biostatistics at the University of Washington, biotechnology at Shoreline Community College, and biomedical informatics at OHSU. He received his BA Biology from Reed College (2011) where he studied [population genetics in cichlid fishes](https://www.reed.edu/biology/professors/srenn/pages/research/2011_students/sean/SM_thesis.html) for his thesis (Renn Lab). He began studying epigenetics and oncology at Fred Hutch ([Grady Lab](https://research.fhcrc.org/grady/en.html), 2015-2018), before enrolling as a Ph.D. student at OHSU in 2018.

Sean has over 5 years of full-time programming experience. He often works with R, Python, and bash on remote server clusters, with advanced utilization of libraries like `ggplot2` and `pandas`, and extensive use of key database syntaxes and backends, including structured query language (SQL) and hierarchical database format 5 (HDF5). His first R software package, [`recountmethylation`](https://www.bioconductor.org/packages/devel/bioc/html/recountmethylation.html), is now available on Bioconductor, a resource for curated and high-quality software that aids biomedical genomics reseach.

Reproducible research in biomedicine and data science is among Sean's key professional interests. He excels at open cross-disciplinary collaborations and software and materials development for research and education. Whether you have an interesting computational problem, or want to discuss reproducibility in science or pursuing a career in bioinformatics, feel free to reach out!
