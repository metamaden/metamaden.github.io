Sean is a bioinformatician, computationalist, and [3rd year Ph.D. candidate](https://www.ohsu.edu/people/sean-maden), Computational Biology advised by Prof. Abhi Nellore, in the Biomedical Engineering Department at Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU). His affiliations include [PDXgx](https://github.com/pdxgx) (Thompson and Nellore lab groups), and his research focuses on study of disease pathology, genomics, epigenetics, and biological aging using whole-methylome and integrative approaches.

Sean's education includes graduate-level courses in bioinformatics, biostatistics, and epidemiology. He received his BA Biology from Reed College (2011) where he [quantified genetic diversity in cichlid fishes](https://www.reed.edu/biology/professors/srenn/pages/research/2011_students/sean/SM_thesis.html) for his thesis advised by Prof. Suzy Renn. He began studying epigenetics and oncology with [Grady Lab](https://research.fhcrc.org/grady/en.html) at Fred Hutch in 2015 before enrolling as a Ph.D. student at OHSU in 2018.

Sean has over 5 years of full-time programming experience for research. His first R software package, [`recountmethylation`](https://www.bioconductor.org/packages/devel/bioc/html/recountmethylation.html), is now available on Bioconductor, a resource for curated and high-quality software for genomics reseach. Sean frequently uses R, Python, and bash on remote server clusters. He makes frequent use of key software packages (e.g. `ggplot2` and `pandas`) and many of the most powerful and ubiquitous database technologies (e.g. structured query language [SQL], MongoDB, and hierarchical database format 5 [HDF5]). To aid reproducibility, Sean versions his projects using Git and GitHub, and he documents his code and findings using programmer notebooks, Markdown, and LaTeX.

High-impact reproducible research is among Sean's key professional interests. He excels at cross-disciplinary collaborations, and enjoys presenting his research to diverse audiences. Whether you have an interesting computational problem or just want to discuss pursuing a career in bioinformatics, feel free to reach out!
