Sean is a bioinformatician, computationalist, and [3rd year Ph.D. candidate](https://www.ohsu.edu/people/sean-maden), Computational Biology, in the Biomedical Engineering Department at Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU). His advisor is Prof. Abhi Nellore, and his research affiliations include [PDXgx](https://github.com/pdxgx) (Thompson and Nellore lab groups) and the [recount](https://jhubiostatistics.shinyapps.io/recount/) team at Johns Hopkins (includes the Leek, Langmead, and Hansen lab groups). Overlapping bioinformatics, biostatistics, and computation, Sean's research focuses on disease pathology, epigenetics, and biological aging. He has contributed integrative analyses of large genomics datasets in numerous publications, and he possesses expertise in whole-methylome analyses using DNA methylation arrays.

Sean's college education includes coursework in evolutionary biology, population genetics, bioinformatics, and biostatistics. He received his BA Biology from Reed College (2011) where he [quantified genetic diversity in cichlid fishes](https://www.reed.edu/biology/professors/srenn/pages/research/2011_students/sean/SM_thesis.html) for his thesis advised by Prof. Suzy Renn. He began studying epigenetics and oncology with [Grady Lab](https://research.fhcrc.org/grady/en.html) at Fred Hutch in 2015 before enrolling as a Ph.D. student at OHSU in 2018.

With over 5 years of full-time programming experience, Sean frequently uses R, Python, and bash on remote server clusters for research. He uses key software packages, such as `ggplot2` and `pandas`, and works extensively with database syntaxes and backends, such as structured query language (SQL) and hierarchical database format 5 (HDF5). To aid reproducibility, Sean makes a habit of versioning his work with Git and GitHub, and documenting his findings using programming notebooks, Markdown, and LaTeX. Sean's first R software package, [`recountmethylation`](https://www.bioconductor.org/packages/devel/bioc/html/recountmethylation.html), is now available on Bioconductor, a resource for curated and high-quality software that aids biomedical genomics reseach.

High impact reproducible research is among Sean's key professional interests. He excels at cross-disciplinary collaborations, and enjoys presenting his research to diverse audiences. Whether you have an interesting computational problem or want to discuss pursuing a career in bioinformatics or data science, feel free to reach out!
