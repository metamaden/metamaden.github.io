Sean is a bioinformatician, computationalist, and [3rd year Ph.D. candidate](https://www.ohsu.edu/people/sean-maden), Computational Biology, in the Biomedical Engineering Department at Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU). His advisor is Prof. Abhi Nellore, and his research affiliations include [PDXgx](https://github.com/pdxgx) (Thompson and Nellore lab groups) and the [recount](https://jhubiostatistics.shinyapps.io/recount/) team at Johns Hopkins (includes the Leek, Langmead, and Hansen lab groups). Sean's research focuses on disease pathology, epigenetics, and biological aging. He has experience with integrative analyses of large genomics datasets and expertise in whole-methylome analyses using DNA methylation arrays.

Sean's education includes graduate-level courses in bioinformatics, biostatistics, and epidemiology. He received his BA Biology from Reed College (2011) where he [quantified genetic diversity in cichlid fishes](https://www.reed.edu/biology/professors/srenn/pages/research/2011_students/sean/SM_thesis.html) for his thesis advised by Prof. Suzy Renn. He began studying epigenetics and oncology with [Grady Lab](https://research.fhcrc.org/grady/en.html) at Fred Hutch in 2015 before enrolling as a Ph.D. student at OHSU in 2018.

Sean has over 5 years of full-time programming experience for research. His first R software package, [`recountmethylation`](https://www.bioconductor.org/packages/devel/bioc/html/recountmethylation.html), is now available on Bioconductor, a resource for curated and high-quality software that aids biomedical genomics reseach. Sean frequently uses R, Python, and bash on remote server clusters. He makes frequent use of key software packages (e.g. `ggplot2` and `pandas`) and many most powerful and ubiquitous database technologies (e.g. structured query language [SQL], MongoDB, and hierarchical database format 5 [HDF5]). To aid reproducibility, Sean versions his projects using Git and GitHub and documents his code and findings in notebooks with Markdown and LaTeX.

High-impact reproducible research is among Sean's key professional interests. He excels at cross-disciplinary collaborations, and enjoys presenting his research to diverse audiences. Whether you have an interesting computational problem or just want to discuss pursuing a career in bioinformatics, feel free to reach out!
