Sean is a bioinformatician, computationalist, and [3rd year Ph.D. candidate](https://www.ohsu.edu/people/sean-maden), Computational Biology, in the Biomedical Engineering Department at Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU). His advisor is Prof. Abhi Nellore, and his research affiliations include [PDXgx](https://github.com/pdxgx) (Thompson and Nellore lab groups) and the [recount](https://jhubiostatistics.shinyapps.io/recount/) team at Johns Hopkins (includes the Leek, Langmead, and Hansen lab groups).

### Background

Sean received his BA Biology from Reed College (2011) where he studied [population genetics in cichlid fishes](https://www.reed.edu/biology/professors/srenn/pages/research/2011_students/sean/SM_thesis.html) for his thesis (Renn Lab). He began studying epigenetics and oncology at Fred Hutch ([Grady Lab](https://research.fhcrc.org/grady/en.html), 2015-2018), before enrolling as a Ph.D. student at OHSU in 2018.

### Research

Sean's research incorporates bioinformatics, biostatistics, and computation, focusing on topics in disease pathology, epigenetics, and biological aging. Much of his work consists of integrative cross-study analysis, or meta-analysis, of large genomics datasets. He has expertise studying DNA methylation, gene expression, and clinical data from many major genomics data resources.

### Programming

Sean has over 5 years of full-time programming experience. He often works with R, Python, and bash on remote server clusters, with advanced utilization of the `ggplot2`, `pandas`, `HDF5`, and `RSQLite` libraries. He often works with many major database syntaxes and backends, including SQLite, MongoDB, and HDF5. Sean's first R software package, [`recountmethylation`](https://www.bioconductor.org/packages/devel/bioc/html/recountmethylation.html), is available on Bioconductor, a curated resource for analysis of high-throughput genomics data.

### Interests

Sean is interested in doing reproducible research to tackle high-impact problems in genomics and data science. He excels at open cross-disciplinary collaborations and development of software and materials for research and education. Whether you have an interesting computational problem, or want to discuss reproducibility in science or pursuing a career in bioinformatics, feel free to reach out!
