Sean is a bioinformatician, computationalist, and [3rd year Ph.D. candidate](https://www.ohsu.edu/people/sean-maden), Computational Biology, in the Biomedical Engineering Department at Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU). I'm advised by Prof. Abhi Nellore, and affiliations include [PDXgx](https://github.com/pdxgx) (Thompson and Nellore lab groups) and the [recount](https://jhubiostatistics.shinyapps.io/recount/) team at Johns Hopkins (includes the Leek, Langmead, and Hansen lab groups).

### Background

Sean received his BA Biology from Reed College (2011) where he studied [population genetics in cichlids](https://www.reed.edu/biology/professors/srenn/pages/research/2011_students/sean/SM_thesis.html) for his thesis (Renn Lab). He began studying epigenetics and oncology at Fred Hutch ([Grady Lab](https://research.fhcrc.org/grady/en.html), 2015-2018), before enrolling as a Ph.D. student at OHSU.

### Research

Sean's research incorporates bioinformatics, biostatistics, and computation, focusing on topics in disease pathology, epigenetics, biological aging, and oncology. Much of his work consists of integrative cross-study analysis, or meta-analysis, of large genomics datasets. He studies DNA methylation, gene expression, and clinical data from many major genomics data resources.

### Programming

I have over 5 years of full-time programming work experience in research. My work typically incorporates R, Python, and bash using remote server clusters running CentOS or Linux, with advanced utilization of the `ggplot2`, `pandas`, `HDF5`, and `RSQLite` libraries. My first R software library, [`recountmethylation`](https://www.bioconductor.org/packages/devel/bioc/html/recountmethylation.html), is available on Bioconductor, a curated resource for analysis of high-throughput genomics data.

### Interests

I'm generally interested in opportunities to do reproducible research tackling high-impact problems in data science. I excel at open cross-disciplinary collaborations, and development of software and utilities for research and education. Whether you have an interesting computational problem, or want to discuss reproducibility in science or pursuing a career in bioinformatics, feel free to reach out!
